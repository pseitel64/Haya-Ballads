Kaiyula

Muzee of Kanazi

Introduction

Kaiyula is a woman's name. It is also the distant thunder that accompanies the first sprinklings of rain after the long, parched, and sometimes hungry dry season.

The ballad of Kaiyula is a poetic evocation of human passion: the supernatural heat that melts together lovers created only for each other; and a lover's crazy, aesthetic, mind-altering embrace of the other's beauty. That is, the ballad is about attraction between youths, on one hand, and between adults, on the other.

Comparing this ballad with The Place You Come From reveals similar episodes based on particular speech interactions: seduction, pillow talk, and post-coital exchange. Kaiyula also has several other kinds of episodes and includes two seductions not one; The Place You Come From gives more emphasis to the post-coital exchange and its consequences. Seeing the episodes as types assists understanding of the many poetic figures and the rhetorical functions they serve.

Muzee sang this ballad on August 22, 1969, to an audience gathered at the traditional house (msonge) of Dionies Lutabanzibwa.
